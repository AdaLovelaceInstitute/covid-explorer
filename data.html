Key insights

Vaccine passports were rolled out without consensus on how to effectively utilise them. Varying pandemic management approaches have led to differences in countries' policies.

Big countries with regional governmental jurisdictions do not have federal vaccine passport policy. Regional authorities decide whether and how to impose vaccine passport requirements, e.g., Brazil, China and the United States.

Protests against vaccine passports and mobility restrictions

Data source: Ada Lovelace Institute

Countries in dataset

Countries which experience protests

Legend

Country with Protest

Country without Protest

1. Nearly 50% of the countries in our sample had protests against vaccine passports and mobility restrictions.

2. Protests reveal the importance of building public legitimay with strong governance mechanisms and transparent and effective communication strategies.

3. In most cases, anti-vaccine passport protests started amid mandatory domestic vaccine passport schemes.

4. This demonstrates the public resistance against using data-driven technology to control mobility and restrict freedoms.

Not every news article makes an explicit link to vaccine passports, but mandatory vaccine policies have been facililated by digital vaccine passports which allowed authorities to control individual's freedoms and access based on their health status. Governments introduced mandatory vaccine policies after they had rolled out vaccine passports. It was hoped that mandatory vaccine passports would incentivise people to get vaccinated, but as can be seen, different sub-groups in society protested this approach and resisted mandatory vaccien passport policies.
