Key insights

1. More than 50% of the countries in our sample had protests against vaccine passports and mobility restrictions.

2. Protests reveal the importance of building public legitimacy with strong governance and transparent and effective communication strategies..

3. In most cases, anti-vaccine passport protests started following the announcement of mandatory domestic vaccine passport schemes.

4. This demonstrates public resistance to using data-driven technology to control mobility and restrict freedoms.

The media did not always explicitly link protests with vaccine passports. However, mandatory vaccination policies were made possible by digital vaccine passports, which allowed authorities to control an individualâ€™s freedoms and access based on their health status. It was hoped that the vaccine passports would incentivise people to get vaccinated, but different sub-groups in society protested this approach and resisted mandatory vaccine passports.
