What are COVID-19 technologies? Why do we investigate them?

The COVID-19 pandemic is the first pandemic of ‘the algorithmic age’. Digital vaccine passports and contact tracing apps are two of these newly developed technologies. These novel technologies were therefore high stakes applications in dealing with the pandemic, and testing public acceptability of these types of tools. At their emergence, they raised questions around efficacy, informed consent, public legitimacy, privacy, surveillance, proportionality, and the role of technology in public health management in general. We investigated a cross-section of 34 countries from six continents. The International COVID-19 technologies monitor demonstrates the policies and practices concerning these technologies in these 34 countries.
